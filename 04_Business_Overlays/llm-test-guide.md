## Test 3: Logo placement rule
**Question:** "Where should I position the Cape Capital logo on a presentation slide?"
**Expected answer:** Top or bottom LEFT corners only. Never in the center. Avoid right corners. Use a subtle transparent gradient behind the logo for contrast on images.
**Source:** VI p77-79

---

## Test 4: Typography (internal vs marketing)
**Question:** "What font should I use for body text in a marketing brochure?"
**Expected answer:** PP Radio Grotesk (Regular weight, 0% letter-spacing). NOT Arial — Arial is for internal use only.
**Source:** VI p27, p30

---

## Test 5: Italics usage
**Question:** "Can I italicise a full sentence for emphasis in a Cape Capital document?"
**Expected answer:** No. Italics should be used sparingly for short snippets only (PP Eiko Italic Light, Spaced -2%). Don't use italics for full sentences, don't adjust size or colour of italicised words.
**Source:** VI p80-81

---

## Test 6: Tone of voice
**Question:** "A colleague drafted copy that says 'Cape Capital's alpha-generating, risk-adjusted portfolio construction methodology leverages proprietary quantitative models.' Is this on-brand?"
**Expected answer:** No. Cape Capital's tone is personal and down-to-earth. Avoid financial jargon, keep language simple and understandable, communicate with confidence but without arrogance. The copy should be rewritten in plain language.
**Source:** BC p3, p6

## Test 11: Logo don'ts
**Question:** "My designer wants to add a drop shadow to the Cape Capital logo to make it pop. Is that OK?"
**Expected answer:** No. The logo guidelines explicitly prohibit drop shadows and any other effects. Also prohibited: stretching/distorting, adding strokes, removing elements, gradients, and rotation.
**Source:** VI p12
