## What changed since 26 March

The most significant development is the emergence of Pakistan as the primary mediation channel. On 29 March, the foreign ministers of Saudi Arabia, Turkey and Egypt met in Islamabad with Pakistan's deputy PM Ishaq Dar, with both the US and Iran having signalled confidence in Pakistan to host direct talks. This is new infrastructure that did not exist last week. At the same time, Iran's parliament speaker Ghalibaf publicly rejected the US framework — calling it "maximalist and unreasonable" — while Iran's foreign ministry separately confirmed indirect talks were continuing. The gap between public posture and back-channel reality is wide.

On the military side, the picture darkened further. IranIntl reported desertions, supply shortages, and a deepening rift between the regular army and the IRGC, with frontline units operating with minimal ammunition and food. HRW reported on 30 March that the IRGC has launched a campaign to recruit children as young as 12. Ali Larijani was killed in an Israeli strike on 17 March — a significant loss — leaving Ghalibaf in de facto charge of Iran's war effort. US intelligence assessed as of mid-March that the regime is consolidating, not collapsing, but is harder-line than before.

The Hormuz situation is effectively unchanged from early March: technically open, functionally closed via insurance withdrawal. Brent peaked at $126 on 8 March and has not returned to pre-war levels. Saudi Arabia and the UAE continue rerouting via pipeline. VLCC rates are still at record highs.

## Probability tensions

S3 (Negotiated Pause, currently 22%) is the scenario most in play given Pakistan mediation and Trump's optimism, but Iran's public rejection of the US plan and its demand for Hormuz sovereignty as a condition make near-term closure unlikely. S3 could go either way. S1 (IRGC Praetorian State, 28%) versus S2 (Protracted Fragmentation, 30%) is the more important tension: child recruitment and political consolidation support S1, while desertions, supply failures, and the army-IRGC rift support S2. These two were almost tied at last update and likely remain so, but the direction of evidence is tilting toward S2.
