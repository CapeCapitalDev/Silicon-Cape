### 1. MARKET SIZE AND PRODUCTION

### 2. BENCHMARKS: CRUDE, BRENT, WTI

### 4. SHALE: TERMINOLOGY AND TECHNOLOGY

### 5. OPEC AND OPEC+
- What is OPEC? When was it founded and why? Who are the current members?
- What is OPEC+? Who joined and when? What is Russia's role?
- How does the production quota system work?

### 6. THE VALUE CHAIN
- Explain the full oil and gas value chain from extraction to end consumer
- Upstream: exploration and production — who does this?
- Midstream: transportation, pipelines, tankers, storage — who controls this?
- Downstream: refining, petrochemicals, retail fuel — who controls this?
- Where are the highest and lowest margins in the chain?
- What is a vertically integrated major vs a pure-play E&P company?

### 7. NATIONAL OIL COMPANIES vs INTERNATIONAL OIL MAJORS

### 8. COMMODITY TRADERS

### 9. PRODUCERS UNDER SANCTIONS

- Iran: what sanctions are in place, what is the estimated production impact, and how does Iran sell its oil despite sanctions (China route, shadow fleet)?

- Venezuela: current sanctions status, production levels, and trajectory

### 10. EUROPE: US LNG vs RUSSIAN PIPELINE GAS

### 11. PIPELINES AND INFRASTRUCTURE

- How does pipeline dependency create geopolitical leverage?

### 12. REFINING AND OIL PRODUCTS

  Include: gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, fuel oil, naphtha, LPG, asphalt
- Why does jet fuel command a premium? What makes it different from diesel?
- What is the "crack spread" and why do refiners care about it?
- Who are the world's largest refiners?

### 13. BY-PRODUCTS AND DOWNSTREAM MATERIALS
- What important industrial and chemical products are derived from oil and gas beyond fuel?
- Petrochemicals: plastics, fertilisers, synthetic fibres — give scale in USD and tonnes
- Helium: how is it produced from natural gas, why is it critical for semiconductors and MRI machines, and which countries dominate supply?
- Phosphates and fertilisers: what is the connection to natural gas (nitrogen fertilisers via Haber-Bosch process)?
- Bitumen and asphalt: scale and importance for infrastructure
- Lubricants, waxes, solvents: brief overview

### 14. OIL AND TAX REVENUE
- What percentage of total government tax revenue comes from oil and gas in: France, Switzerland, United States, United Kingdom?
- For each country, break this down: (a) direct taxes on oil companies (corporate tax, windfall taxes), (b) fuel/excise duties paid by consumers, (c) royalties and production levies
- How has this percentage changed over the last 20 years? Is oil becoming more or less important to government finances in each country?
- Which countries globally are most dependent on oil revenues as a share of total government income (e.g., Saudi Arabia, UAE, Norway, Russia, Nigeria)?

- How does Switzerland differ from France and the UK — is there meaningful oil-related tax revenue at all?
- What is the fuel duty escalator in the UK and France — how much of a litre of petrol at the pump is tax?

### 15. TECHNOLOGY: EXTRACTING MORE FROM EXISTING WELLS
- What percentage of oil in a reservoir is typically recovered using conventional techniques? What is the industry term for this (recovery factor)?
- What is Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR)? Explain the three main methods: thermal, gas injection (CO₂, nitrogen), and chemical (polymer flooding)
- What is the typical uplift in recovery rate from EOR — from what baseline to what improved rate?
- Which technologies are currently extending the productive life of ageing fields? Include: horizontal drilling, multilateral wells, 4D seismic, AI-driven reservoir modelling, downhole sensors
- Which companies or regions are leading in EOR deployment?
- What does it cost to apply EOR per barrel, and at what oil price does it become economic?
- **End-of-well-life environmental hazards:**
  - What are the main environmental risks when an oil well or field reaches the end of its productive life?
  - What is well abandonment — what does proper decommissioning involve and what does it cost?
  - What happens when wells are improperly abandoned — methane leakage, groundwater contamination, subsidence?
  - How many orphaned or abandoned wells exist globally, and what is the estimated liability?
  - Who is legally responsible for decommissioning — the current operator, original licensee, or state?
  - What are the North Sea decommissioning costs and timeline? Who bears them?
  - Are current decommissioning provisions (financial reserves set aside by companies) adequate?

### 16. OIL EXPLORATION: NEW METHODS AND ECONOMICS

- **Exploration economics:**
  - What is the average cost to drill an exploration well — onshore vs offshore vs deepwater?
  - What is the average success rate for exploration wells (commercially viable discovery)?
  - What is the average cost per barrel of proved reserves discovered over the past decade?
  - How much are the majors (Shell, BP, Exxon, TotalEnergies) spending on exploration vs. prior decades — and is exploration capex rising or falling?
  - What is the average time from discovery to first oil — how has this changed?
- **Deepwater and frontier:**
  - What are the main deepwater basins currently being developed (Guyana, Brazil pre-salt, Mozambique)?
  - What does it cost to develop a deepwater field vs. a conventional onshore field, per barrel of production?
