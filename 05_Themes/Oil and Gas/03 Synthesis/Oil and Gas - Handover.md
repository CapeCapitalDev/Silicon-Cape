## Key Findings to Carry Forward
- The oil market has two simultaneous realities: a structural bear case (~$55-60 Brent, driven by 3.84 mb/d surplus + shale maturity + slowing demand) and an acute geopolitical spike ($114+) from the Hormuz closure. The medium-term base case is that the bear case reasserts when the crisis resolves.
- The shale era of volume growth is definitively over — operators themselves say so in filings. US production plateaus at ~14 mb/d. The marginal cost of US shale ($48-70 full-cycle) is effectively the structural ceiling on oil prices absent geopolitical disruption.
- The petrodollar transmission — oil shock → CPI → Fed constraint → USD strength → EM stress → demand destruction — is running. Investors positioned only for spot price miss the sequentially available macro trades over 12-24 months.
- Non-combustion petrochemical demand (fertilizers, helium, plastics, pharma, synthetic rubber) is the durable, inelastic floor under oil demand that the energy transition cannot remove. This is structurally underappreciated in transition narratives.
- Qatar's Ras Laffan single-point-of-failure risk is materially underpriced: one facility produces LNG + petrochemicals + 25-30% of global helium. The Iran conflict has already triggered a partial shutdown.
