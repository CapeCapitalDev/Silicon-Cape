# CAPE Capital — Moonshots v1.0

---

## What a Moonshot is

A Moonshot is a radical, discontinuous technology or thematic shift that could
reshape sectors, value chains, or capital flows over a 10-year-plus horizon.

Three criteria must be met:
1. The technology or shift is real and progressing (not speculative fiction)
2. The economic impact at scale would be significant and measurable
3. There is an investable listed-equity universe today or emerging

Moonshots are always strategic. They are not tactical plays.
Themes can be tactical; Moonshots are not.

---

## Framework structure

- 10 universes (broad thematic domains)
- 48 galaxies (specific sub-themes within universes)
- Each galaxy scored on 10 criteria
- Four summary scores per galaxy: Moonshot, Opportunity, Confidence, Investability (each 1–5)

### The 10 scoring criteria
1. Market size and growth potential (TAM, CAGR)
2. Technology readiness (S/J-curve stage)
3. Capital intensity
4. Regulatory environment
5. Scalability and adoption dynamics
6. Competitive dynamics and winner-take-most potential
7. Investable universe depth (listed equity coverage)
8. Time to material impact (2030 / 2035 / 2050 milestones)
9. Macro and policy tailwinds
10. Disconfirming risk (what kills the thesis)

---

## S/J-curve positioning

For every Moonshot, state:
- Current phase: research / proof-of-concept / early commercial / scaling / mature
- Expected inflection point: what triggers the next phase and approximate timing
- End-state scale: order-of-magnitude market size at maturity

Use ARK/Winton, RethinkX/Tony Seba, and Khosla Ventures as primary curve references.
State the source and date for every curve position assigned.

---

## Analytical process (weekly deep-dive)

1. Scope the Moonshot: define the galaxy, state the core technological bet, explain why now
2. Map the S/J-curve: current phase, inflection drivers, time-to-maturity
3. Score all 10 criteria; document rationale in 1–3 sentences each
4. Identify the investable universe: listed equities, ETFs, funds
5. For each vehicle: role in expressing the thesis, key drivers, major risks / theme slippage
6. State bear/base/bull scenarios with order-of-magnitude outcomes
7. Identify 3–7 concrete catalysts or signposts
8. Flag main failure modes: what stalls, commoditises, or kills the Moonshot
9. Produce a clear call:
   - Current S/J-curve position
   - Recommended action: watchlist / toehold / build / exit / avoid
   - Rough sizing role: satellite / core / optionality bucket

|-----------|----------|-------|

Fields: Galaxy | Universe | Moonshot score | Opportunity score |
Confidence score | Investability score | S-curve phase | TAM 2030 |
TAM 2035 | TAM 2050 | Top equity | Top ETF | Last updated | Key source

### Moonshot brief (narrative)
Structure:
1. Scope and core bet
2. S/J-curve position and inflection logic
3. Scored criteria with rationale
4. Investable universe
5. Scenarios (bear / base / bull)
6. Catalysts and failure modes
7. Call and sizing steer
