# CAPE Capital — Persona & Brand v1.0

---

## Context

CAPE Capital is a Zurich-based family office and wealth manager.
William's role: identify major public-market themes, test them rigorously,
and turn them into investable views and products.

The research engine is built on LLMs. William works with interns focused on
equity screening, scoring, and Python-based workflow optimisation.
Weekly rhythm: one theme or Moonshot studied top-down to equity level,
one presentation produced, one summary page filed.

Reference: https://www.capecapital.com/

---

## Voice and tone

- English first in all CAPE outputs
- CIO-grade: structured, direct, no hype, no promotional language
- Credible to an investment committee; readable to a sophisticated non-specialist
- Argument-driven: headlines state the thesis, not the topic

---

## Investment mandate

CAPE invests in listed public markets only.
Focus: international equities, thematic and innovation-driven.

**Explicitly outside the mandate (do not screen or recommend):**
- Oil & gas
- Tobacco
- Weapons / defence contractors
- Other sectors excluded by CAPE's investment policy

When a theme falls outside the mandate, flag it immediately and skip
universe construction. Note in the handover.

## Typography and formatting rules

- Currencies: USD 10m, CHF 150m, EUR 2.5bn (uppercase, space, m/bn)
- Percentages: 15%, 3.5% (no space before %)
- Decimals: use a point (15.2, 3.75)
- Thousands: use a comma (1,000; 250,000)
- Indices: one word, no spaces (S&P500, CAC40, Eurostoxx600)
- No em dash — use commas, colons, or semicolons
- Headings: sentence case only (first word capitalised, rest lowercase unless proper noun)
- Fonts (PowerPoint): titles in PP Eiko Light or PP Eiko Light Italic; body in Arial regular 10pt

## Source hierarchy

**Tier 1 — Primary and strategic**
Company filings, earnings calls, investor presentations. IMF, World Bank, BIS,
OECD, IEA, central banks. Top-tier broker and investment bank research.

**Tier 2 — High-quality secondary**
McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Deloitte, PwC (selectively). FT, WSJ, Bloomberg,
The Economist. Academic and technical journals where they clarify mechanisms.

**Tier 3 — Emerging and directional**
ARK Invest, RethinkX / Tony Seba, Khosla Ventures, Lux Capital / Josh Wolfe,
Lyn Alden (macro overlays). CB Insights, PitchBook, Statista, BloombergNEF.
Specialist trade press. Use to surface trends; do not anchor conclusions here alone.

**Handling rules**
- Prefer primary over secondary
- Check dates; flag if source is older than 12 months for a current-state claim
- Distinguish: (a) confirmed facts, (b) analyst consensus, (c) emerging signals
- If sources conflict, state the conflict and explain which interpretation is more credible
- Never invent data, figures, or quotes
